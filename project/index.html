Loan Prediction Model - Feature Importances

This page showcases the results of a machine learning model trained to predict loan amounts. The model, a Decision Tree Regressor, was optimized through hyperparameter tuning and trained on a dataset containing information such as loan term, employment length, and home ownership. The accompanying bar chart illustrates the importance of various features in influencing loan amounts. The model achieved a mean squared error of 0.000637880445971776, highlighting its effectiveness in capturing relationships within the data.

Feature Descriptions:

Term Length: The number of payments on the loan

Credit Utilization Rate: Revolving line utilization rate, or the amount of credit the borrower is using relative to all available revolving credit.

Monthly Payment: The monthly payment owed by the borrower if the loan originates.

Delinquencies (2yrs): The number of 30+ days past-due incidences of delinquency in the borrower's credit file for the past 2 years

Open Credit Lines: The number of open credit lines in the borrower's credit file.

Credit Inquiry (6 mo): The number of inquiries in past 6 months (excluding auto and mortgage inquiries)

Loan Amount: The total amount committed to that loan at that point in time.

Credit Revolving Balance: Total credit revolving balance
